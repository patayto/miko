An exploration of [[Claude Code]] usage limits and cost optimization strategies triggered by first-time hitting of daily limits. The author investigates token efficiency through model selection (Haiku vs Sonnet vs Opus), discovers [[Claude Projects]] and content caching as primary limit-management tools, and learns about [[Retrieval-Augmented Generation]] (RAG) as an emerging feature for handling larger knowledge bases within Projects.

Key findings on usage patterns: different subscription tiers (Pro vs Max) offer 5-20x higher allocations; messages reset every 5 hours; and message capacity depends on length, file attachments, conversation history, and tool usage. The author identifies that [[Claude Projects]] provides automatic caching of reused content, reducing token overhead, and that RAG enables Projects to handle vastly more context while maintaining response speed.

Secondary exploration of directory listing techniques via `wget` and robot.txt/sitemap.xml discovery, and recognition that AI SDKs (Python and TypeScript) are emerging as critical infrastructure for building software with Claude models.
