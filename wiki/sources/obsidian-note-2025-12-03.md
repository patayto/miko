A raw stream-of-consciousness from December 2025 working through depression, the need for new goals, and the question of whether to build one's own enterprise or serve another's.

## The Four-Path Frame

The author opens by naming four options available when depression and ideation are chronic: end it, continue as-is, change the self (habits like pessimism, rumination), or change the situation. The last carries the highest effort and uncertainty — yet the people who dismiss it as impossible are the same people who claim individuals can make a difference. That contradiction disqualifies their advice.

## Self-Portrait

A detailed accounting of capabilities: intelligence (widely attested, including by a philosophy professor), strong memory (possibly photographic), pattern recognition, ability to explain complex topics to non-specialists, acute perceptiveness. Counter-forces are also named honestly: fear of others' intentions, risk of manipulation, diminished humour since depression deepened, recent withdrawal from any pursuit. [[Authentic Wants]] are felt but buried.

## The Patron Fantasy

Across multiple eras — hunter-gatherer, ancient Greek, medieval Irish, 18th-century court — the author imagines themselves as scout, scholar, shaman, oracle, wizard: valued for knowledge, protected by a powerful patron, free to pursue their own research. This is the [[Patron Model]] of meaningful work. The author notes the illusion clearly: they would likely have revolted against the constraints, the patron would have owned their output and potentially killed them for failing to deliver the impossible.

## Amazon as the Modern Castle

[[Amazon]] is recast as the king's castle in present form. The "protection" was salary and relocation to California. The author built an alchemical tool (data into money) and was rewarded with investment. Then dependency on weed, missed work, declining performance — eventually the author quit and returned home. In the metaphor: the apprentice was dismissed and replaced before they could be executed outright. See also [[Amazon Career Narrative: Predictive Demographics and Demographic Targeting]].

## The Elphaba Moment and the Rundown Castle

The author identifies with Elphaba post-Wizard: having seen the system for what it is, having sung "Defying Gravity," having quit — and now standing in their own castle which is rundown and needs renovation. The central question is whether to renovate it (build their own thing), seek another king's court, or do nothing.

## Body vs Mind on Timing

A persistent split: the body wants to build *now*, immediately, without waiting. The mind says: we have no bricks, no plans, no land. Get resources first. This maps onto [[Motivational Conflict]] and the tension between urgency and strategic patience. The author acknowledges they would ask 100 future selves the same question and all would say: be slow and patient.

## Shoot for the Moon

The author concludes they want billionaire-CEO-level freedom and resources — not corporate executive comfort and monotony. They know the odds are near zero. They decide to aim there anyway, framing guaranteed comfort as a kind of death: if dying is the alternative, attempting the impossible in the interim costs nothing extra. This is a direct expression of [[Authentic Wants]] and [[Depression and Ideation]] intersecting.
