Interstitial journaling is a practice of writing timestamped notes continuously throughout the day, in the gaps between tasks and activities, rather than reserving journaling for a dedicated session. Each entry is brief and contextual — a thought, a decision, a task completed, a conversation noted — and is tied to the moment it occurs.

The core benefit is reduced friction in capture. Because notes go into a single chronological stream rather than into pre-named folders or topic pages, the writer does not need to decide where something belongs at the time of writing. Organization happens retroactively through linking, tagging, or querying.

[[LogSeq]] is a natural fit for interstitial journaling: the daily journal page is the default landing surface, the `/time` [[LogSeq Slash Commands|slash command]] inserts a timestamp inline, and the block-linking system allows any captured thought to be surfaced later by topic.

The practice is especially useful for:
- **Meeting notes** — timestamped entries tie context to a specific moment in a conversation
- **Task tracking** — logging what you worked on and when, building a retrospective record
- **Thought capture** — fleeting ideas get written down before they disappear, without filing overhead

Interstitial journaling pairs well with [[Time Blocking]] in that it documents what actually happened during blocks, rather than just what was planned. The output over time is a dense, searchable record of daily cognitive activity.
