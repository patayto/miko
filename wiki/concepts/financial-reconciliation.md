Reconciliation is the process of matching expected transactions against actual account activity to identify errors, unauthorized charges, or billing anomalies.

Regular reconciliation catches several categories of billing problems:

**Unexpected charges**: A subscription cancellation may not fully prevent future charges; third-party billing platforms (like Rocket Money) can surface charges that appear on bank statements but not in subscription management interfaces.

**Duplicate transactions**: Financial events that appear to have duplicated (e.g., a final vehicle loan payment charged twice) require investigation and reversal support.

**Authorization gaps**: Services may continue charging after cancellation due to timing issues between cancellation and billing cycles, or due to miscommunication between customer and vendor.

Tools like Rocket Money and Chase transaction history provide external verification of actual account flows. When reconciliation reveals discrepancies, customer support escalation is required, documented by transaction dates and amounts.

Systematic reconciliation is particularly important around account transitions (cancellation, service relocation, device changes) where the likelihood of state mismatch between the customer's intent and the vendor's systems increases.
