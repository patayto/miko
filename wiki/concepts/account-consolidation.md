Managing multiple email accounts for different services creates operational friction, security risk, and billing confusion.

Account consolidation seeks to reduce the number of active identities, each of which requires password management, recovery procedures, and ongoing maintenance. A common pattern involves having both a legacy and a primary email address with overlapping service subscriptions.

The costs of duplication include:

**Financial**: Duplicate subscriptions (e.g., two separate [[iCloud+]] accounts with different renewal dates) represent money spent on redundant access. Consolidation saves recurring costs and simplifies billing tracking.

**Operational**: Remembering which services are attached to which email address creates friction during recovery scenarios, password resets, and billing disputes. Each account doubles the surface area for account takeover.

**Recovery**: Services may be partially stuck after account migration. For example, an app might become un-login-able after losing access to the recovery phone number used to set up the account, as can happen with international relocation.

Effective consolidation typically involves:
- Choosing a primary email address for future services
- Gradually migrating existing services to the primary account
- Managing overlapping subscriptions during the transition period
- Keeping secondary accounts in minimal-access state or fully retiring them

The process reveals which subscriptions are truly needed versus which are artifacts of account history.
