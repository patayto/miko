Managing recurring subscriptions requires continuous visibility and periodic auditing to prevent waste and catch billing errors.

Effective subscription management involves three layers:

**Inventory**: Maintaining a complete list of active subscriptions across all accounts and providers. Many subscriptions accumulate unconsciously, and account duplication (as seen with [[Apple]] iCloud+ services) can create unintended redundancy. Tools like Rocket Money provide external perspective on actual charges.

**Rationalization**: Periodically questioning whether each subscription still delivers value. Services like Calm, Duolingo, and Headspace can offer trial benefits that wear off; explicit cancellation decisions reduce passive spending.

**Reconciliation**: Matching actual charges (from bank statements) against expected subscriptions. Billing errors—such as unexpected charges after cancellation—require swift investigation and customer support escalation.

International relocation introduces complications: subscriptions may become geographically ineligible (as with Veo's US-number requirement), and international coverage for services like [[AppleCare+]] requires explicit verification.

Cost optimization often means evaluating whether specialized insurance or bundled offerings (generic tech insurance vs. per-device plans) offer better value than à la carte subscriptions.
