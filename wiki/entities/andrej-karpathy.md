AI researcher and engineer known for foundational work in deep learning and neural networks. Created the [[LLM-wiki]] architecture (available as a [GitHub gist](https://gist.github.com/karpathy/442a6bf555914893e9891c11519de94f)), which inspired the author's personal knowledge management system.

Karpathy's llm-wiki approach is notably non-prescriptive about implementation details but establishes non-negotiable structural elements: `raw/` (read-only sources), `wiki/` (output), `CLAUDE.md` (agent instructions), `index.md` (catalog), and `log.md` (append-only timeline). Key insights include the value of consistent log entry formatting for machine-parseable timelines and the principle that "good answers can be filed back into the wiki as new pages."

The author references Karpathy's work when considering multi-context knowledge organization, operations design, and agent specialization for different source domains.
